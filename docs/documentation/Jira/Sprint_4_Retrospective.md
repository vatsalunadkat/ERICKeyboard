# ERICK — Sprint 4 Retrospective

**Sprint**: SCRUM Sprint 4  
**Dates**: March 16–20, 2026 (Monday–Friday)
**Velocity**: ~32 story points planned  
**Sprint Goal**: Polish keyboard features, integrate game controller support, implement word prediction foundation

---

## 👍 What Went Well

1. **Core tasks progressing smoothly** — Most members completed their development on time. Function integration did not disrupt existing system functions. Both the keyboard and game modules yielded substantial results.

2. **Obvious functional optimization impact** — The backspace/word switching logic, word box prompts, error feedback, save/load system, and other features were all successfully implemented. User experience was significantly improved.

3. **High-quality task definitions** — Jira tasks had clear structure and definite steps. Even if a member was absent from the planning meeting, they could easily get started, making task execution smoother.

4. **Focused team goals and improved proficiency** — The team's goals were more focused, proficiency in tools and engines improved, members could handle tasks independently and assist teammates, and collaboration efficiency was higher.

5. **Structured bug troubleshooting** — Specification for bug troubleshooting and reporting processes enabled team members to record issues in a structured manner and effectively identify logical and visual defects.

6. **High engagement on final polish** — All team members had a high level of engagement, focused their efforts on polishing the final version, and the finished product achieved a high degree of completion.

---

## 👎 What Didn't Go Well

1. **Cross-module collaboration costs** — There were communication costs in cross-module/cross-system collaboration. Edge scenarios required repeated confirmation, and integration was not smooth enough.

2. **Equipment and environment issues** — Some members' computers were incompatible with the engine's rendering method, requiring time to troubleshoot environmental faults, which affected progress.

3. **Enormous final integration workload** — Code merging within the keyboard, within the game, and between the keyboard and game for joint debugging was highly difficult and time-consuming. The overall rhythm of the final iteration was rather chaotic. Some high-difficulty tasks could not be completed on time, bringing pressure and frustration to team members.
